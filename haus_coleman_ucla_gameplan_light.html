Making it work
Practical notes
You don't have to commit to the certificate yet
UCLA Extension allows up to 5 courses before establishing candidacy. Start with Fundamentals of Interior Architecture this fall — pay the $745, take the class, see how it fits your life. No commitment required.
Let Haus of Coleman fund UCLA
One Blueprint client at $700 covers almost a full course. Two clients covers it with money left over. The business is designed to pay for the education that makes the business more valuable. They feed each other.
Skip quarters without guilt
You have 5 years. If a quarter is hard — grief, M.Ed pressure, a big project — skip it. The program accommodates you. Rest is not falling behind. It is staying in the game long enough to finish.
Use every course in your marketing
The moment you enroll in a course, add it to your Instagram bio, your brochure, and your email signature. "Currently studying Interior Architecture at UCLA Extension" is a powerful line. You don't have to be done to be credentialed.
History courses can be taken out of sequence
The four History of Environmental Arts courses are flexible — take them in any order, in any quarter. They're lower-intensity and make great "rest quarter" courses when you still want to stay enrolled but need lighter work.
The M.Ed is not a conflict — it's a credential stack
M.Ed in Leadership + UCLA Interior Architecture + 15 years experience + your story = a profile nobody else has. When you're ready to teach design or lead a program, that combination opens doors that neither degree opens alone.

This is not a race to a certificate. It is a deliberate, self-funded education that makes you more valuable every single quarter — whether or not you're enrolled that term. You are already a designer. UCLA just gives you the language, the credentials, and eventually the license to prove it to anyone who asks.
