Client Conversation Scripts
What to say, and when
Script 1 · When someone asks "what do you offer?"
Lead them to your best offer
"I offer a few different ways to work together, but most people start with The Blueprint. It's a complete design plan for your space — layout, sourcing, color, everything — so you can execute it at your own pace without the overwhelm."
You didn't list everything. You led them to your best offer. That's the move.
Script 2 · Discovery conversation
Three questions that do all the work
"Tell me how your space feels right now… and what you wish it felt like instead."

"What have you already tried that didn't quite land?"

"Are you looking for a full transformation, or more of a reset using what you already have?"
Gets their emotional language · shows expertise · positions you as translator, not decorator.
Script 3 · Positioning your value
The line that separates you from 90% of designers
"What I do is take what you're describing — especially the parts that are hard to articulate — and turn it into a clear, intentional design that actually reflects you. Most people don't need more options. They need direction."
Say it once. Then let it land. Don't add to it.
Script 4 · Pricing
Say it clean. No apology.
"For The Blueprint, I'm at $300 per room. My minimum is two rooms, so the space feels cohesive. That's $600 to start."
Then STOP. Do not explain. Do not justify. Do not fill the silence. The silence is working for you.
Script 5 · If they hesitate on price
Hold your positioning. Don't chase.
"Totally understand. Most of my clients come to me after trying to figure it out on their own and realizing they want it done right the first time. If now isn't the right time, that's completely fine."
This does NOT chase them. It keeps your positioning intact. Walk away with your dignity and your rate.
Script 6 · Closing
Simple. Direct. No overtalking.
"If this feels aligned, I can send over the intake form and we can get started this week."
That's the whole close. Don't add another sentence.
Script 7 · When someone overextends you
The boundary, held warmly.
"That would fall outside of this package, but I'm happy to add it on if you'd like."
Warm. Clear. Non-negotiable. Every time.
