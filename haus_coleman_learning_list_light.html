Couch-Mode Learning List
Everything below can be done lying down, phone in hand, no effort required. This is not a to-do list — it's a menu. Pick up whatever feels good. Put it down whenever you want.

YouTube

Watch from bed

Carmeon Hamilton — Nubi Interiors
Search: Carmeon Hamilton interior design
A Black Memphis-based designer and content creator who documents her full design process. Her perspective on cultural identity in spaces will feel like a direct conversation with your own brand values.
Black designerCultural designProcess-focused
ISTYLEMYSTYLE — Sheryl
Search: ISTYLEMYSTYLE YouTube
A Black designer focused on budget-conscious styling and thrifting. Deeply practical and warm. Great for learning how to talk about accessible luxury — which is one of your content pillars.
Black designerBudget stylingThrifting
Studio McGee
Search: Studio McGee YouTube
High-end residential design documented beautifully. Watch for how they present client projects, talk about their process, and build a brand narrative. Good study in the business side of design.
Client processBrand buildingHigh-end residential
Emily Henderson Design
Search: Emily Henderson Design YouTube
Her "Why This Room Works" series is one of the best free design education resources available. She breaks down exactly why a space succeeds — proportions, color, balance — in plain language.
Design theoryRoom analysisPractical education
Architectural Digest — Open Door series
Search: Architectural Digest Open Door
Celebrity home tours that are genuinely educational. Train your eye to notice how high-end designers layer a space — textiles, art, scale, light. This is the "watching aspirational content as research" category.
AspirationalEye trainingLuxury reference

TikTok

Scroll with purpose

Rasheeda Gray — Gray Space Interiors
@rasheedagray
Black woman designer known for transitional modern chic. She openly shares both her design work and her entrepreneurial journey — including the hard parts. Exactly the kind of voice Haus of Coleman will one day be.
Black woman designerEntrepreneur mindsetTransitional style
Demetrius — At Home with Savvy
@athomewithsavvy
Black designer who built a large following through elevated everyday styling. Good study in how to grow an audience by showing lifestyle and design together — not just finished rooms.
Black designerElevated lifestyleAudience building
Kayla Simone Home
@kaylasimonehome
Black woman designer with a strong point of view and good vibes. She bridges DIY and design in a way that is relatable without being cheap — useful to study as you think about accessible luxury content.
Black woman designerDIY meets designAccessible luxury
Sandy Santilus
Search: Sandy Santilus TikTok
Atlanta-based Black designer who started from sharing her own apartment transformation. Her renter-friendly, cost-conscious approach is proof that a relatable origin story is a brand asset, not a liability.
Black designerOrigin story brandBudget-friendly

Instagram

Follow and absorb

Beth Diana Smith
@bethdianasmith
Featured in Forbes and Wall Street Journal. A former finance professional who pivoted to design and built a powerhouse firm. Her layered textures and maximalist color palettes are stunning — and her story mirrors yours in some ways.
Black woman designerCareer pivot storyMaximalist
Shavonda Gardner — SG Style
@sgstyle
Eclectic global style, bold color, intentional living in small spaces. Her work on identity and living large with less is philosophically close to what Haus of Coleman is building toward.
Black woman designerGlobal influenceBold color
Malene Barnett — Black Artists + Designers Guild
@malenebarnett
Founder of the Black Artists + Designers Guild. Follow her not just for design inspiration but for community — this organization exists specifically to support Black creatives in the design world. Worth knowing about.
Black woman designerCommunity + advocacyArt + design
Sheila Bridges
@sheilabridges
One of the most influential Black designers working today. Elle Decor A-List since 1994. Her Harlem Toile wallpaper is in the Smithsonian. Follow her to study what it looks like when cultural identity becomes iconic design.
Black woman designerCultural identity in designLegacy

Articles + Blogs

Read when your brain wants depth

Architectural Digest — ad.com
architecturaldigest.com
The gold standard. Read their home tours for compositional study, their trend pieces for market awareness, and their designer profiles for business inspiration. Free on the web.
AspirationalTrend awarenessFree
Apartment Therapy
apartmenttherapy.com
The practical counterpart to AD. Great for accessible design ideas, before/after stories, and understanding what everyday clients want. Read their "Small Cool" and "House Tours" sections.

RMCAD — Color Theory in Interior Design
rmcad.edu/blog
A genuinely useful free article that explains how color affects mood, emotion, and perception room by room. This is the kind of thing that will sharpen your client consultations and your content voice.
Color theoryEducationalFree
Coco Cozy — Black Interior Designers on TikTok

A roundup specifically highlighting Black designers on TikTok with links to each account. A useful discovery tool — read it once and you'll have a week's worth of new accounts to explore.
Black designersDiscovery listQuick read

One thing that isn't design

But is entirely for you

Black Artists + Designers Guild
badguild.com — founded by Malene Barnett
An organization founded specifically to amplify Black artists and designers in spaces that have historically excluded them. Worth exploring their membership and community — not for networking pressure, but to know you are not building this alone. There are rooms full of people who have done what you are doing.
