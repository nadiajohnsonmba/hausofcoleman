The Foundation
What this brand is
Haus of Coleman was born from grief, built on legacy, and rooted in a lifetime of understanding how spaces hold people. Its founder brings fifteen years of event and floral design — the art of making rooms feel like moments — and the soul of a musicologist who grew up understanding that the greatest art doesn't just sound good. It means something. That is what every Haus of Coleman space is built to do.
01 · The Record
Tactile. Intentional. Ritual.
A great room, like a great record, rewards the ones who take the time to be present in it. Every texture, every surface, every material is chosen to be felt — not just seen.
02 · The Sample
Old made new. Yours.
We take what already exists in a space — its bones, its light, its history — and reinterpret it into something that couldn't belong to anyone else. The greatest rooms, like the greatest tracks, are built from what came before.
03 · The Cypher
Culture. Community. Circle.
Design is not neutral. Haus of Coleman creates spaces that celebrate Black identity, honor cultural lineage, and make everyone who enters feel like they belong in the room. We show up in the circle.
04 · The Liner Notes
Details for those who listen.
The Calacatta tray. The weight of the curtain fabric. The exact angle of the mirror. Most people won't name what they notice — they'll just feel that something is different here. The liner notes are for the ones paying attention.
05 · The Drop
The moment everything hits.
Every Haus of Coleman project builds toward a reveal — the moment the room becomes itself. The drop is not decoration. It is the reason for every decision that came before it.
06 · The Frequency
The invisible thing that changes everything.
You walk into a room and something shifts in your body before your eyes adjust. That is frequency — the feeling a space transmits. Haus of Coleman designs for the frequency first. Everything else follows.

My brother was one of the most respected DJs of his generation — a man who understood that music doesn't just fill a room. It transforms it. He was born in Germany, took his music to every corner of the world, and was my best friend until I lost him in 2020. Haus is German for house — a nod to where he began.

Coleman is our mother's name. I was building this brand in secret, waiting until it was ready — until I could walk in and show her something whole, something worthy of carrying her name. She passed 25 days after I started, before I ever got the chance to tell her. She never knew it existed.

But I built it for them. And I carry them both into every space I design — the music, the memory, the frequency that never really goes away.

That is what Haus of Coleman is built on.
